Japandi style everywhere, even in the bathroom

In recent years, the Japandi style has taken the world of interior design by storm, blending Japanese elegance with cosy Scandinavian functionality. Although these two worlds may seem far apart, they share a love of simplicity and a preference for natural materials and colours, creating environments that convey serenity and harmony.

What is the Japandi style?

Japandi combines two design philosophies: Japanese minimalism and functional Scandinavian design. This hybrid style creates tidy, clean and simple environments characterised by a sophisticated and relaxing atmosphere.

Its aesthetic combines purity of lines with practicality, creating harmonious and welcoming spaces.

The main features of the Japandi style include:

  1. Minimalism: clean lines and minimalist character for an essential aesthetic.
  2. Natural materials: warm-toned woods are a must for both furniture and flooring. Other materials that can be used include bamboo, wicker, steel, glass and ceramics.
  3. Neutral colours: this style is characterised by a palette of colours found in nature, such as white, beige and grey. Dark tones can also be used but in combination with colours such as green and blue. Finally, black details are typical of Japanese tradition.
  4. Functionality: every detail has a functional and decorative role. All unnecessary accessories should be avoided.
