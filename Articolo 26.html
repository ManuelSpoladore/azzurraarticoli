Designer mirrors: irregular shapes are the norm!

The old cliché of simple, rectangular mirrors is now just a faded memory. The new trend that is taking interior design by storm is mirrors with unusual and irregular shapes, which are true works of art, perfect for embellishing and enriching spaces with a touch of originality and style.

Mirrors have always played a fundamental role in interior design. In addition to their practical function, they often have the valuable advantage of visually enlarging spaces, brightening the environment and adding a sense of depth to rooms.

As design and taste have evolved, countless types of mirrors have appeared, from the opulent and ornate Baroque frames to the simplicity and minimalism of recent decades. Recently, however, irregular and creative shapes have become increasingly popular, transforming mirrors from purely functional objects into decorative elements.

The success of irregular shapes for designer mirrors

The mirror thus becomes a piece of furniture, a focal point and the absolute protagonist of the room. So, forget about linear contours and make way for sinuous shapes, asymmetrical profiles and unexpected frames.

Round, oval, hexagonal and much more: the freedom of geometry gives personality and character to every project. The creativity of architects and interior designers makes them truly unique: mirrors can be hung individually or grouped together in imaginative combinations to create a veritable art gallery on the walls of your home. Furthermore, a combination of mirrors of different shapes and sizes can also be very effective.

In addition to their aesthetic appeal, unusual mirrors play an important functional role. Strategically placed, they can help you conceal other unattractive elements of the room or emphasise interesting architectural details. All this while continuing to perform their main task: reflecting natural or artificial light, making spaces brighter and more welcoming.
