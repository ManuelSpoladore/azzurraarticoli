How to organise your bathroom with a pet

If you have a four-legged friend at home, you know that sharing space with them requires some precautions. These include bathroom furnishings, a space that must be functional, safe and comfortable for both you and your pet.

In this article, we will give you some tips on how to make your bathroom pet-friendly without sacrificing style and design.

The shower: how to bathe your pet

Bathing your pet can be a challenge, especially if your pet doesn't like water or is too big or heavy to lift. To make this task easier and more enjoyable, you can follow these tips:

  • Choose a walk-in shower, which eliminates any kind of tub wall and allows you to easily get in and out with your pet.
  • Use a detachable shower head, which allows you to move the water spray around your pet and reach all parts of its body.
  • Place a non-slip mat in the shower to prevent you or your pet from slipping and hurting yourselves.
  • Use a special shampoo for your pet that is gentle on its skin and coat and does not contain any irritants or toxic substances.
  • Dry your pet thoroughly after bathing, using a soft towel or a hairdryer on a low setting, to prevent them from getting sick or making the house wet.

Bowls: where to put your pet's water and food

Your pet needs a dedicated area for feeding and drinking. The bathroom can be a practical solution, as it allows you to have running water at your fingertips and to clean the bowls easily.

However, you must take care to choose a suitable place, which should be:

  • Away from the toilet, shower and sink to avoid contamination and splashing.
  • Stable and accessible, to prevent the bowls from tipping over or moving around.
  • Clean and hygienic, to prevent the spread of bacteria and mould.

You can use a cabinet, shelf or rack where you can place the bowls safely and conveniently. Remember to change the water often and wash the bowls thoroughly to ensure your pet's health.

Toys: how to entertain your pet in the bathroom

Your pet also needs to have fun and burn off its energy.

The bathroom can be a suitable place for your pet to play, if you have enough space and your pet is not at risk of damaging or swallowing dangerous objects.

You can create a play area in the bathroom where you can put their favourite toys, such as balls, scratching posts, soft toys or ropes. You can also use organisers that hang on the walls or cabinet doors to keep toys tidy and always within reach.

If your pet loves water, you can also bathe them in the bathtub or shower using a detachable shower head and a non-slip mat. However, remember to dry your pet thoroughly after bathing to prevent them from getting sick or getting the whole house wet.

Design: how to furnish your bathroom with style and functionality

Having a pet at home does not mean sacrificing taste and aesthetics. You can furnish your bathroom with style, choosing accessories that are in harmony with your pet and the rest of the house. For example, you can choose:

  • A pet bed that matches the colour of the walls or furniture, or that has a particular shape or material.
  • Bowls with an original design or personalised with your pet's name or photo.
  • Fabrics or rugs that are durable, washable and hypoallergenic, to create a warm and welcoming atmosphere.
  • Photos, prints or paintings depicting your pet, to make your bathroom unique and original.
