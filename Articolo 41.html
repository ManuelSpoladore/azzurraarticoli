How to choose colours for bathroom furnishings

Color is one of the most important elements in bathroom decor because it influences the style, atmosphere, and perception of the space. Choosing the right colors for bathroom furnishings is not always easy, as several factors need to be considered, such as size, lighting, materials, and finishes.

In this article, we’ll give you some tips on how to choose and combine colors for your bathroom decor, while avoiding the most common mistakes.

1. Follow Your Style

The first tip is to follow your personal style when decorating your bathroom, by choosing colors that best suit your personality and taste. There are no right or wrong colors for the bathroom, only colors that reflect your preferences. For example, if you love a classic style, you can go for white, beige, gray, or black, whose shades always guarantee an elegant and refined effect. If you prefer something more original, you can be bold with brighter and more vivid tones, such as red, green, blue, or yellow — perfect for expressing energy and vibrancy.

2. Don’t Overdo the Color Variety

Try not to use too many different colors in the same bathroom, as this can result in a chaotic and unbalanced look. To avoid this issue, you can follow the "60-30-10 rule":

  • Choose three colors that complement each other;
  • Use one of them—preferably the most neutral—for 60% of the space;
  • Use the most contrasting color for 30% of the decor;
  • Finally, use the remaining color sparingly 10% to highlight certain details.

3. Balance Color Intensity

Avoid using colors that are all too intense or too dull together, as this can create an unpleasant and tiring effect.

4. Consider the Size of the Bathroom

Your color choice should also depend on the available space, since colors significantly affect the visual perception of the environment. As a general rule:

  • Light colors help make the space appear larger;
  • Dark colors should be used only to create focal points or emphasize details;
  • You can use glossy or reflective surfaces to bounce light and enhance the sense of space.

5. Match Bathroom Furniture to Wall Colors

One last aspect not to overlook is matching the bathroom furniture with the wall colors to create a final effect of perfect harmony.

As mentioned earlier, there’s no single rule — the final choice depends largely on your personal taste. The important thing is to rely on the experience of specialized retailers to ensure you achieve the desired effect in your project.
