3 DIY anti-limescale remedies for cleaning the bathroom

Are annoying limescale deposits your biggest problem when cleaning your bathroom? Would you prefer to avoid using harsh chemicals? Here are three easy and eco-friendly DIY limescale remedies for sparkling results every time.

Before revealing them, however, we would like to remind you that each material can react differently and, before using any detergent or substitute, it is important to check the instructions for the product to be cleaned and always test the solution on a small area of the surface before using it extensively.

Now we're ready to show you some natural remedies for fighting limescale.

1. Vinegar

White vinegar is a real ally when it comes to fighting limescale in the bathroom. Preparing a diluted solution of vinegar and water is very simple. Just pour a little into a bowl and apply it with a soft sponge to surfaces such as taps, sinks and showers. Leave the vinegar to work for a few minutes to dissolve the limescale, then rinse with warm water. For stubborn taps, unscrew the filters and soak them in a solution of water and vinegar overnight. You'll be amazed by the results.

2. Baking soda

Another powerful ingredient against limescale is baking soda. If you want to make your sink shine, scrub it with half a lemon and baking soda. This combination not only removes limescale but also leaves a pleasant, fresh scent. You can also mix baking soda and white vinegar, which is ideal for cleaning shower stalls: apply the mixture to the surfaces with a sponge, leave it to work for a few minutes and rinse thoroughly.

3. Citric acid

If you have more delicate surfaces, such as marble or wood, citric acid is the best choice. Dissolve it in water and apply it with a sponge to the areas affected by limescale. Leave it to work for a few minutes, then rinse. This remedy is particularly gentle on more sensitive surfaces and is just as effective.

The super combination: bicarbonate of soda, vinegar and lemon

If you have particularly stubborn limescale, the combination of bicarbonate of soda, vinegar and lemon is your trump card. Mix three parts baking soda with one part warm vinegar and add the juice of one lemon. This powerful mixture will not only dissolve limescale, but will also have a sanitising effect, eliminating bacteria and germs. You can use this combination on more resistant surfaces, but be sure to rinse well after application.
