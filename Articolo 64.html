Minimalist bathroom: a design that improves mental wellbeing

In a fast-paced world, design is also constantly evolving. Trends that seem indispensable today are already outdated tomorrow. Yet there is one style that does not follow fashion, but transcends it. It is essential, elegant and timeless. We are talking about minimalist design, an aesthetic philosophy that revolutionises your spaces, but can also improve your mental well-being.

Because living in a simple, tidy, essential environment also means living with less stress and more clarity.

Minimalism is more than a philosophy, it is a way to redefine your environment, starting with your furnishings.

What is minimalism?

Starting out as an artistic movement, minimalism quickly left the art galleries and entered our activities, our wardrobes and, more generally, our homes. The most famous phrase that embodies this philosophy is “Less is more”, attributed to modernist architect Ludwig Mies van der Rohe. A simple, almost minimalist principle, but a powerful one: remove the superfluous to enhance what really matters.

Minimalism means choosing. It means freeing yourself from visual noise, both mental and material, to make room for simplicity. It means prioritising quality over quantity. Even on a scientific level, research shows that less chaotic environments, with fewer objects and stimuli, are associated with lower levels of cortisol (the stress hormone) and greater mental clarity.

A simple step towards adopting a minimalist lifestyle is to redefine your environment: even your home can reflect this new vision. Simple, functional furnishings help the mind to remain free and focused.

Start with the bathroom, a place often cluttered with products and accessories that we accumulate without any real need for daily use. Making it tidy and harmonious could be a turning point in always feeling tranquillity in the room where you start and end your days.

Features of a minimalist bathroom

The space in a minimalist bathroom is organised in a rational way. The use of suspended furniture and clean lines frees up the floor and makes the room look tidier.

There are no handles, which are replaced by recessed or push-pull opening systems, maintaining maximum visual cleanliness. The colours follow a neutral palette, ranging from white to light grey to dove grey or pastel shades. The finishes can be soft touch, giving a soft and delicate feel.

The mirror, a functional and decorative element, follows the same minimalist approach: simple, rectangular or round shapes, with integrated LED lighting around the perimeter or backlighting. The washbasin has essential shapes, often integrated into the top or countertop, with smooth, thin surfaces in ceramic or composite materials. Finally, the sanitary ware also follows the same style: it is compact, suspended and with a flush-to-wall drain, thanks to space-saving solutions such as the Wally system by Azzurra. The shapes are rounded or slightly square, for a light and tidy look.

Why choose a minimalist design?

Choosing a minimalist design has numerous practical and aesthetic advantages. Here are three reasons why it can make a difference in your bathroom:

1. Simplicity and sophistication

Choosing a minimalist design allows you to create an environment free from distractions. Aesthetics are not sacrificed; on the contrary, this style gives elegance to the bathroom as well as making it more practical. In fact, clean lines, natural materials and careful choices lend refinement to the bathroom. Your guests will notice it right away!

2. Add value and functionality to the room

With a minimalist design, every element has a purpose. This means that spaces and objects are enhanced and anything unnecessary is removed. This makes the room easy to live in, love and clean with ease.  For example, wall-mounted sanitary ware makes it easier to clean the floor.

3. Create a sense of calm

Fewer objects, less chaos. Minimalist design reduces visual noise and promotes a feeling of calm and cleanliness. Space influences our mood, and an environment based on the essentials can make a difference to our well-being.
