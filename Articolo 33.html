How to choose towel rails for the bathroom

To make your bathroom comfortable and tidy, it is essential to choose your accessories carefully, starting with towel rails. These items are not only practical, but also aesthetic, helping to create the desired atmosphere and style.

But how do you choose the most suitable towel rails for your bathroom? What criteria should you follow? Here are some useful tips to guide you in your choice.

Types

There are different types of towel rails, which vary in shape, size and installation method. Some of the most common are:

  • Wall-mounted towel rails: these are fixed to the wall near the sink, bidet or shower. They can be single or double, flag-shaped or ring-shaped. They are the classic solution for keeping towels within easy reach, but they have the disadvantage of requiring holes to be drilled in the wall for installation.
  • Towel rails integrated into bathroom furniture: these can be placed at the front or side of the countertop with sink, depending on the structure and design of the furniture. This is an aesthetically pleasing and space-saving solution.
  • Floor-standing towel rails: these rest on the floor and can be moved as desired. They come in a variety of shapes, from minimalist and linear to more creative ladder designs. They are perfect for adding a touch of originality to your bathroom.

Placement

The choice of towel rack also depends on where you want to place it in the bathroom. You need to take into account the available space, the layout of the bathroom fixtures and the needs of those who use the bathroom.

For example, if the bathroom is small or long, a towel rail under the sink may be ideal, as may a towel rail with a bar parallel to the cabinet.

If, on the other hand, the bathroom is large or has two sinks, you can opt for two single towel rails to be installed next to each sink, or a double or multiple towel rail to be positioned in a strategic location.

For the bathtub or shower area, it is best to choose a towel rack with at least two bars and hooks so that both towels and bathrobes can be hung up.
