Lighting in the bathroom: an aspect that should not be underestimated

How important is a careful study of lighting in bathroom design? The answer is simple: very important.

The quantity and quality of lighting is a truly fundamental element when it comes to bathroom furnishings, as it is for any interior design project. Light is known to influence the well-being, aesthetics and functionality of any environment.

So how do you choose the right lighting for your bathroom? Let's take a look at some aspects that should not be overlooked.

What to consider when choosing bathroom lighting

First, you need to distinguish between natural light and artificial light.

Natural light

Natural light is light that comes from windows or skylights, and is the most desirable because it gives a feeling of space, cleanliness and freshness. It also saves electricity, reducing consumption and environmental impact. To make the most of it, windows should be positioned to ensure good sunlight exposure for as much of the room as possible, avoiding shadows and reflections. It is also advisable to choose reflective materials for the walls, floors and bathroom furniture in order to maximise brightness.

Artificial light

Artificial light comes from lamps or spotlights and is necessary to supplement or replace natural light when it is insufficient or absent. It must be adapted to the different functions of the bathroom, such as using the sink and shower, relaxing or personal care. For this reason, it is essential to create different light sources that can be switched on or off as required. For example, you can install soft lighting to create a relaxing atmosphere in the bathtub, direct lighting to illuminate the mirror, and decorative lighting to highlight certain elements of the bathroom, such as wall units, plants or paintings. Finally, it is important to choose energy-efficient lamps with a colour temperature that is suitable for your bathroom.

By choosing the right combination of natural and artificial light, you can improve your well-being and also make an informed choice to respect the environment.
