How to sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths

Bathroom cleaning cloths are constantly in contact with dirt, limescale, soap residue and bacteria found in the bathroom. If they are not sanitised properly, they can become a breeding ground for bacteria, as well as giving off unpleasant odours and losing their effectiveness, becoming damaged before their time. To avoid these problems, it is important to sanitise them after each use. Let's see how.

How to sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths

There are several methods for sanitising bathroom cleaning cloths, both with chemical products and natural remedies. Let's take a look at some of the most common and effective ones.

Sanitising bathroom cleaning cloths with bleach

Bleach is one of the most commonly used products for disinfecting bathrooms and cleaning tools. It has strong bleaching and antibacterial properties, but it is also very aggressive and can damage fabric fibres and irritate the skin. For this reason, it should be used with caution and diluted in water. To sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths with bleach, simply follow these steps:

  • Fill a bucket with hot water and add a glass of bleach.
  • Immerse the cloths in the bucket and leave them to soak for at least 15 minutes.
  • Rinse the clothes thoroughly under running water and wring them out.
  • Dry the clothes in the open air or in a tumble dryer.

Sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths with vinegar

Vinegar is another natural product that is very useful for household cleaning. It has degreasing, anti-limescale and deodorising properties, and is also an excellent natural disinfectant. To sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths with vinegar, simply follow these steps:

  • Fill a bucket with hot water and add two glasses of white vinegar.
  • Immerse the cloths in the bucket and leave them to soak for at least 30 minutes.
  • Rinse the clothes thoroughly under running water and wring them out.
  • Dry the clothes in the open air or in a tumble dryer.

Sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths with baking soda

Baking soda has descaling, whitening and deodorising properties, and is also a good ally against mould. To sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths with baking soda, simply follow these steps:

  • Fill a bucket with hot water and add three tablespoons of baking soda.
  • Soak the cloths in the bucket and leave them to soak for at least an hour.
  • Rinse the clothes thoroughly under running water and wring them out.
  • Dry the clothes in the open air or in a tumble dryer.

Sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths with citric acid

Citric acid is a natural substance found in citrus fruits such as lemons, oranges and grapefruit. It has anti-limescale, degreasing and antibacterial properties and is also a good natural preservative. To sanitise bathroom cleaning cloths with citric acid, simply follow these steps:

  • Dissolve five teaspoons of citric acid in 400 ml of cold water.
  • Pour the solution into an empty spray bottle.
  • Spray the solution onto the clothes and leave it to work for a few minutes.
  • Rinse the clothes thoroughly under running water and wring them out.
  • Dry the cloths in the open air or in a tumble dryer.

Other tips for sanitising bathroom cleaning cloths

In addition to the methods described above, there are other useful tips for keeping bathroom cleaning cloths in good condition:

  • Always use different cloths for different surfaces in the bathroom to avoid transferring dirt and bacteria from one place to another.
  • Wash the cloths in the washing machine regularly, using a high-temperature programme and an antibacterial detergent.
  • Add a few drops of tea tree, lavender or lemon essential oil to your detergent or fabric softener to naturally fragrance and disinfect your cloths.
  • Always dry your clothes thoroughly after use, avoiding leaving them damp or crumpled, to prevent mould and unpleasant odours from forming.
